The chart illustrates how the world’s population is distributed across major first-language groups. Mandarin dominates with 30%, while Spanish accounts for 15% and English for 10%. Hindi and Arabic represent 8% and 7% respectively, while the remaining 30% is shared among numerous smaller languages.

The data highlights extraordinary linguistic concentration in a few major languages, while also reflecting significant diversity in the long tail of less widely spoken tongues.

[OVERVIEW]
A handful of major languages account for the majority of speakers.
