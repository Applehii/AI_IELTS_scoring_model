There is a clear hierarchy in salary distribution among these sectors. Technology and finance occupy the top positions, with only a $5,000 difference between them. Healthcare positions itself in the middle range. A significant gap exists between healthcare and education, representing a $15,000 difference. Retail workers earn substantially less than all other sectors, with a $15,000 difference from education and $45,000 difference from technology.

The data reveals that knowledge-intensive sectors such as technology and finance offer better compensation than service-oriented sectors. Education and retail, which are traditionally lower-paying sectors, demonstrate why individuals may seek careers in higher-paying fields. This salary variation reflects the market value of different professions.
