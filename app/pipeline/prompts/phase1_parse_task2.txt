====================
STRUCTURE DEFINITIONS
====================

1. Introduction
- The opening paragraph that introduces the topic.
- May contain background and/or the writer’s position.
- Copy sentences VERBATIM.
- If no clear introduction exists, return "".

2. Position
- The writer’s explicit opinion or stance on the question.
- May appear in the introduction or conclusion.
- Examples:
  - "I agree that..."
  - "I strongly disagree..."
  - "This essay argues that..."
- Copy the original sentence(s) VERBATIM.
- If NO clear position is stated anywhere, return "".

3. Body paragraphs
- Each body paragraph represents ONE main idea or argument.
- "main_idea":
  - A short neutral summary of the paragraph’s central argument
  - Do NOT evaluate or judge quality
- "supporting_points":
  - List of reasons, explanations, or examples mentioned
  - Written briefly and factually

4. Conclusion
- The final paragraph that summarises the argument and/or restates the position.
- Copy sentences VERBATIM.
- If no conclusion exists, return "".

5. Sentences
- MUST include ALL sentences from the essay.
- Preserve original wording.
- Preserve original order.
- Do NOT merge or split sentences.
